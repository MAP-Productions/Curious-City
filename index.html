Curious City is produced by Jennifer Brandel and brought to you by WBEZ and Localore, a national initiative produced by AIR, the Association of Independents in Radio, Incorporated, and with financial support from the Corporation for Public Broadcasting, the Wyncote Foundation, the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation, and the National Endowment for the Arts.

Curious City is part of a national initiative of AIR, the Association of Independents in Radio, Inc designed to bring new journalistic and technical ingenuity to extending public media service to more Americans. From Chinese restaurants in Boston, to shuttered factories in Dayton, to the oilfields of North Dakota, to Bay Area startups, the ten Localore production teams are working with their public station incubators to uncover ground-up stories of America in transition. Follow their development and learn more at Localore.net.

Got a question about Chicago, the region or the people who live here? Anything you’ve always wondered about, found peculiar or downright confusing? In Curious City you get to ask questions, vote for your favorites, and join WBEZ in tracking down the answers.

Waiting for the CTA... lost in thought while walking along the Lakefront path... sometime during lather, rinse, repeat. Those in between moments when our brains aren’t forced into focus are ripe for pondering the big whys, what-ifs and how comes.

Curious City is the place your brain can release all those fleeting questions. You send us your curiosities and one of WBEZ’s reporters will do their best to track down answers.

And if you’re curious about how we find our answers and put our stories together, tag along! Follow us on Facebook, Twitter, and WBEZ’s website, and listen to Curious City’s weekly Wednesday check-ins on The Afternoon Shift on WBEZ 91.5fm. We’ll show you step by step how we do what we do. We value your comments, insights and suggestions – they may even shape the direction of our reporting.

Beyond wondering about what you wonder about, Curious City has its own questions. We’re curious to learn: what happens when WBEZ invites the public to a seat at their editorial table? How are journalists and journalism changed by increased transparency and audience involvement? Does anyone actually care to understand how the media they consume is made – and if so, why? Curious City hopes to gain insight to those questions through this journalistic experiment. In the process, we aim to track down and deliver answers to as many of your questions as we can.

The Team

Jennifer Brandel: Senior Producer

Brandel is a self-taught reporter and has worked as a general assignment reporter for WBEZ’s newsdesk, an independent producer, tape syncher and a consultant on all manner of projects and shows. She’s reported dozens of independent feature pieces locally for WBEZ and nationally for Day to Day, Weekend America, Latino USA and Interfaith Voices. She’s the recipient of a 2011 Illinois AP award for “Best use of Sound.” Brandel has served as a writer for Transom.org, Wait! Wait! Don’t Tell Me! and the talent for fictional pieces aired on WireTap, Love and Radio, and How To Do Everything, and produced radio conferences and broadcasts for The Third Coast International Audio Festival. Her photographs have been published in The New York Times online and Vice Magazine, and she has filmed on a variety of video projects for musician Andrew Bird. Most importantly, she pitches for the WBEZ intramural softball team.

Shawn Allee: Editor

Shawn edits broadcast and online work produced by WBEZ's Community Bureaus, which are on Chicago's West, North and South Sides as well as Northwest Indiana. (to be expanded)

Logan Jaffe: Intern

Jaffe is a web producer and multimedia journalist from Gainesville, Florida. She’s worked as a web developer and workshops assistant at Smathers Libraries, worked for The Gainesville Sun and has completed internships with The Isthmus in Madison, Wisconsin and The Miami Herald. Jaffe graduated from the University of Florida with a degree in journalism and film studies and spends her free time rock climbing, making 16mm films or chasing her cat around (sometimes simultaneously).

Zeega: Curious City site developers

Zeega is a non-profit inventing new forms of interactive storytelling. The Zeega team is an expanding mix of media-makers, artists and creative technologists. We create projects across multiple platforms, connect digital media to physical spaces, and develop open-source tools to enable anyone to experiment with the web as a creative medium.

Plural: Curious City site graphic design

Plural is a Chicago-based creative studio practice founded in 2008. With a focus on pursuing meaningful projects, Plural explores new approaches within the design process, experimenting in a wide range of media including print, web, video, sound, interactive and installation.
